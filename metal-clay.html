Precious Metal Clay (pmc) also known as Art Clay

This is one of the pieces I'm currently working on. I call it 'Dragonfly Wings in Art Clay.' I used a scientific illustration that I created in a class at the Museum of Natural Sciences with Laszlo Meszoly at Harvard. I transferred that illustration to 'Bake and Bend' clay in a class at Metalwerx. I then pressed the Art Clay into the Bake and Bend mold to create my art clay dragonfly. I placed the dragonfly on a medallion I fashioned to look like a fossil. It turns out, the whole piece was too large to use for a piece of jewelry so I broke it up into quarter pieces.

Green Stage

Each of these pieces will become a separate piece of jewelry. The clay is very dry at this stage, so I'm applying water and slip to get other pieces to adhere to it.

Planning Design

I'm planning a pendant. I think I'll hang it from a branch; not the actual branch although that might work too, but to a branch made of art clay. At this point I need to think about how I'll connect the two pieces. I can drill a hole for a jump ring or I can embed an eyelet; one or the other will attach to a chain.

Using Molds

The purplish mold of the twig that you see in this image was created with a Fast-Set Silicone Putty Equinox 35. It has worked ok for me up to this point, but I think I will research one that will pick up more detail.

Cleanup

Trimming extra clay off the branch while the clay is still pliable. I think I might have less clean-up work to do with an epoxy that captures more detail. That's something I will look into.
